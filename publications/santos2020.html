Abstract

This research aims at investigate the means of propagation of the COVID-19/SARS-CoV-2 between individuals during social contact. A small scale model is proposed to investigate the behavior of salivary droplets between individuals who may be contaminated by orally transmitted diseases. This will allow a detailed understanding of the rapid spread of COVID-19 viral load and the effectiveness of containment effects of the disease in our society. The results point that saliva particles both travel forward and stay in the air close to a person who coughs.
